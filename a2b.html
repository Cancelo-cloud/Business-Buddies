  • Review Session: 15 minutes
    • Review filler words, pronunciation, cadence, and engagement.

Take-away: Five thinking behaviors before speaking:

  • Is it clear — could you explain or give an example?
  • It is accurate — could we test the truth of that?
  • It is precise — could you be more specific?
  • It is relevant — does it answer the question or solve the problem?
  • It is logical — does it make sense, is it coherent with evidence?
Thinking and Reasoning Frameworks

Once a week Online: Monday

Training Focus:

  • Personal Reflection Tools
  • Business Thinking Frameworks
  • Human Psychology and Decision Making

Take-away:

  • 1 Cognitive Bias and 1 Mental Model
  • Connect a Bias or mental model into: personal reflection, business frameworks, or understanding human psychology.

Example:

Cognitive Bias: Overconfidence:

  • Personal life: Your friends laugh at your jokes, and you become overconfident, thinking you could be a successful comedian.
  • Business: A person gets an idea and launches a startup without market research, overconfidently assuming everyone thinks like them and will want the product.
  • Human psychology: People consistently believe they are above-average in looks, intelligence, or skill — until reality checks in (like seeing an unexpected photo or video of themselves performing).
